How to identify and interpret symbols on carved graves in the US and Canada

Symbolism is a popular way of communicating specific information about the deceased. The reading of these symbols is now less-common knowledge but knowing what some of the most common symbols means can deepen your visits to the cemetery and tell you more about the people whose lives these stones mark. Symbols vary across time, place, and cultural context and some symbols which mean one thing in a certain place and time will mean something entirely different elsewhere, for example the Buddist swastika and the Nazi hooked cross. In a time when literacy was less common than it is today, the images carved into a stone were as important as the letters in the case of visitor recognition and interpretation.

How to recognize and identify graves not marked by a modern western-style gravestone.

If you are in a non-urban area that has been settled for a long time it is entirely possible that there are burial places outside of sanctioned cemeteries, from graveyards of the enslaved to family cemeteries on family land. Oral tradition is often more correct than the highly-educated like to admit. If locals say that there is an old cemetery no-longer marked in a certain place, then it is very likely there. Certain signs can help to differentiate a graveyard from an ordinary plot of land, such as indentations in the earth and the growth of certain plants, particularly yucca.

How to perform basic gravesite conservation for carved gravestones

A gravestone left uncleaned will deteriorate faster than one that is tended to about once every 7 to 10 years, however many cemeteries do not include this kind of maintenance as a matter of course, particularly older cemeteries. If you would like to perform this task for a family member's grave or even a stranger's it is often welcome. Before beginning, please note that gravestones in truly bad shape should be left to professionals. Do not try to restore broken or tipped stones to their place and do not try to clean badly chipped stones. No matter what condition the stone is in or what permission has been gained or not, it is always a safe and helpful choice to clear and detritus around the base of and on the top of any stone in a public cemetery.
